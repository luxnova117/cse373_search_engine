Moneygall[edit]

User:Odea has added a link to the article on Moneygall against two editors' suggestion that it be excluded. I am going to revert the latest addition of the link to maintain the status quo while we build consensus for the inclusion/exclusion of it here.

Question: Should a link to Moneygall be included in this template?

  • No - While the visit was a major event for the town, it was a minor event outside of that context. We already have List of presidential trips made by Barack Obama and List of presidential trips made by Barack Obama during 2011 where the trip is mentioned. Also, it does not appear on Barack Obama, Presidency of Barack Obama, or Family of Barack Obama. If we include Moneygall, do we include all of the hundreds of other visits he has made? —Diiscool (talk) 13:26, 23 June 2011 (UTC)
  • No per Diiscool. It is WP:UNDUE weight and an inappropriate link as the article itself is not about Obama. – Muboshgu (talk) 14:00, 23 June 2011 (UTC)

You didn't build that meme[edit]

A link to the You didn't build that political attack article has been added to the speeches section, but shouldn't be placed in a section reserved for speeches, even if the phrase was uttered on the campaign trail in a "speech". As listed, it gives the false impression that Obama has a speech called that. It would be better listed under Public image in the "News and political events" or "Miscellaneous" sections. Gobōnobo + c 08:53, 15 September 2012 (UTC)

Agree, the article isn't about his speech, but about the controversy that arose out of that phrase being used as a political football. I've gone ahead and removed it. aprock (talk) 03:57, 12 September 2013 (UTC)
I saw this due to relation to the Trevon Martin speech, yet the Roanoke, Virginia speech is excluded? Should the Trevon Martin speech be removed? Or the Roanoke, Virginia speech be added?--RightCowLeftCoast (talk) 05:19, 14 May 2015 (UTC)
